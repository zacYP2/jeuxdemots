TRISTAN AND JULIET
TRAVEL IN TIME 

1-BATOMA AND JULIAN: Discover differences and similarities between the school days of an African girl and a Canadian boy. Chronological sequencing and association of text cards and photos, descriptive writing, questionnaire. 

2-THE INVENTION MUSEUM: View an exhibition of some unusual inventions, including velcro and the world's smallest organ. Chronological sequencing and association of text cards and illustrations, write-on/wipe-off chart (magic pen included). 

3-A FUN YEAR: Learn about new and familiar seasonal festivals throughout the year. Three-way association and sequencing of 12 text cards with illustrations and names of months. 

4-CHILDREN OF THE WORLD: Interesting facts about the daily lives of children throughout the world. Multiple choice questions, decoder, creative extension activities. 

5-A DAY IN MY LIFE: Complete a personal schedule to determine and compare how two different 24-hour days are spent. Charting using reusable vinyl stickers, calculating and comparing.

TRISTAN AND JULIET 
ROAM THROUGH SPORTS

6-A CYCLING TOUR: Travel along with a group of youngsters on a fun-filled, overnight bicycle trip. Matching illustrated snap-shots to corresponding text cards, questionnaire. 

7-A SPORTING DAY: Help organize and schedule a school-wide Field Day, and select a nutritious lunch menu. Charting with reusable vinyl stickers, selecting text cards that illustrate literary differences of content and style. 

8-BIG SPORTS: Fold-out comic book follows a group of friends on a mountain hiking trip. Completing dialogue balloons with reusable vinyl stickers, questionnaire. 

9-A BICYCLE RIDE: Follow the safety rules on an adventure-packed bicycle trip through town. Mapping skills, storytelling. 

10-LET'S DRIBBLE: Fold-out comic book tells the story of a group of friends who organize their own basketball teams. Completing dialogue balloons with reusable vinyl stickers, questionnaire. 

Up

TRISTAN AND JULIET 
RIDE WITH ANIMALS

11- STAMP COLLECTING: Learn about endangered animals as Jennifer shares her Canadian wildlife stamp collection. Matching stamp reproductions to descriptions, identifying common characteristics of all stamps, creative extension activities. 

12-WHICH IS WHICH?: Discover the characteristics that distinguish similar wild animals from each other (alligator and crocodile, dolphin and porpoise, etc.). Labeling and matching illustrations of sixteen animals to corresponding descriptions, determining and listing additional pairs of animal twins, naming male and female of species. 

13-THE FOOD CHAIN: Learn about the food chain by helping ten hungry animals choose the restaurant that serves the foods they eat (herbivore, carnivore, omnivore). Charting with reusable vinyl stickers, determining and listing additional animals from each alimentary group. 

14-WHALES: Discover interesting facts about these endangered giants of the sea. Labeling illustrations of seven types of whales with reusable vinyl stickers, questionnaire. 

15-WHAT IS A BIRD?: Learn about the shared characteristics of all birds, and the unique qualities of some. Multiple choice questions, decoder. 

Up

TRISTAN AND JULIET 
DISCOVER NATURE 

16-THE HERBARIUM: Identify the distinguishing characteristics of seven species of deciduous and coniferous trees.Matching leaf illustration cards to description cards. 

17-LIFE IS GREEN: Determine personal ecological habits.Self-discovery quiz. 

18-OPEN, TRASH CAN: Learn all about recycling.Sorting and classifying illustrations of recyclable and non-recyclable items, write-on/wipe-off word search (magic pen included). 

19-THE GREEN THUMB: Become the school expert on houseplants and reply to four "Dear Green Thumb" letters. Matching student letters to illustration of selected houseplants, write-on/wipe-off word search (magic pen included), creative extension activities. 

20-A CANOE TRIP: Develop mapping and orientation skills on a canoe trip in the wilderness.Story and comprehension questions. 

Up

TRISTAN AND JULIET 
DRIVE, NAVIGATE, AND GLIDE 

21-MEANS OF TRANSPORT: A brief history of the bicycle and public transportation.Labeling twelve illustrated vehicles with reusable vinyl stickers. 

22-TRAVELING: A letter home describes means of transportation around the world. Multiple choice questions, decoder, creative extension activities. 

23-QUEBEC CITY: A short history of one of the oldest cities in North America. Matching postcard photos to captions, mapping, riddles. 

24-LAND, SEA, OR AIR: Discover some unusual means of transportation from ancient to modern times. Labeling illustrations of unusual vehicles with reusable vinyl stickers, write-on/wipe-off sentence completion (magic pen included), dictionary suggested. 

25-MY BICYCLE: Identify the many parts of an ordinary bicycle! Write-on/wipe-off diagramming and sentence completion (magic pen included). 

Up

TRISTAN AND JULIET 
HAVE FUN TRAVELING 

26-TANGRAM: The history of this ancient Chinese puzzle and related activities. Durable plastic Tangram included. 

27-POSTCARDS: Roberto shares his collection of postcards depicting famous monuments from around the world. Matching ten illustrated postcards to corresponding captions, sequencing. 

28-VACATION SOUVENIRS: Rebecca shares ten souvenirs from her trip around the world. Multiple choice matching of souvenir to country of origin, decoder, creative extension activities. 

29-YARD SALE: Six friends put on a yard sale. Decipher clues and a secret code to determine the items contributed by each friend. Decoding, write-on/wipe-off lists (magic pen included), mathematical extension activities. 

30-MY PASTIMES: Magazine-format self-discovery quiz considering how leisure time is spent. Multiple choice questionnaire.

TRISTAN AND JULIET 
LEAD THE INVESTIGATION 

31-A THEFT AT THE LIBRARY: Follow the clues and discover the identity of a rare book thief! Select correct photo out of four suspects, decode numerical secret message, complete newspaper article. 

32-ACE OF HEARTS: Follow the clues and locate a stolen race horse! Identify suspects using Gumshoe Files, choose appropriate newspaper article, decode cryptic secret message. 

33-ALI-BABA: Uncover an illegal counterfeiting operation while cave exploring! Decode cuneiform-style secret message, use dictionary, select pertinent newspaper article, write letter to police. 

34-PAPRIKA: Follow the clues and discover who stole a precious vase from the museum! Decode secret message, use Gumshoe Files and Identikit strips to determine suspect, complete newspaper article. 

35-MYSTERY AT THE CIRCUS: Go on assignment and find out who is causing so much trouble at the circus! Use Identikit strips to construct possible suspects, encode alphabetical secret message, choose correct newspaper article.

TEACHER'S GUIDE 

The Teacher’s Guide describes the material, the learning objectives, the procedures to follow and makes different suggestions for using the material.

STUDENT NOTEBOOK 

The child-friendly structure of the Student Notebook enables children to organize their written responses as they work independently, while allowing the teacher to easily evaluate individual progress. Compact and attractive, teh Student Notebook is highly recommended when using the complete ANIMALS/BLUE SERIES as a whole-class activity. 

WORKSHOP WHEEL 

The Workshop Wheel is a helpful tool for organizing Activity Workshop sessions in the classroom. With a spin of the numbered inner wheel, students divided into groups 1 through 7 can quickly identify the Activity Workshop symbol corresponding to their team. 

Diameter: 43.5 cm (17")
